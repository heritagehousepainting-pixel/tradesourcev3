## The Moment TradeSource Started

TradeSource started with a five-figure repaint job on a homeowner's house.

I had sold the job easily. Before the homeowner even met me in person, she was ready to stop looking at other painters and hire me. The sale was not the issue. The problem came after the job started.

At the time, I was relying heavily on the same subcontractor I had been using for my work. I trusted that setup and did not think I needed to keep building more labor relationships in the background.

On the second day of the job, that subcontractor did not show up.

Suddenly I was stuck.

I was on a major job, alone, trying to keep the project moving by myself while also scrambling to find labor. I was on Facebook Marketplace, searching Google, and driving to local paint stores looking for business cards. I was calling people I knew nothing about, just hoping someone would answer and be usable.

That was the breaking point.

---

## What Felt Broken

The labor search process felt completely broken.

If you need help fast in the trades, the options are usually bad:

- panic-search Facebook Marketplace
- search Craigslist
- call random numbers from Google
- collect business cards from supply stores
- hope word of mouth turns something up in time

None of that is a real system.

You do not know who you are calling.
You do not know if they are reliable.
You do not know if they do quality work.
And if you go after the wrong labor, you either lose money, damage the homeowner relationship, or both.

I remember sitting in my truck thinking there should be an easier way to find reliable subcontractors.

Not random crews.
Not expensive crews found through pay-to-play search results.
Not people trying to charge the same rates I was charging, leaving no room for profit.

I wanted access to the crews that are actually out there — the ones who just want good jobs put in front of them so they can show up and work.

---

## The Bigger Realization

At the same time, I was thinking about other contractors and friends in the trades who were dealing with the same kind of problem.

A lot of small operators are good at marketing and sales. They can get the homeowner, close the deal, and win the contract. But they do not always know where to turn when it comes to labor.

They either:
- overbuild a staff too early
- rely too heavily on one or two people
- or panic when labor falls through

Around that same period, I was also talking to a restaurant owner who told me about a large painting company in Philadelphia that was actively looking for subcontractors because they were so busy.

That was the moment everything connected.

On one side, there were contractors with more work than they could handle.
On the other side, there were capable crews who wanted work.
And in between, there was no trusted system connecting them.

That is when TradeSource came together in my mind.

---

## The Core Insight

The problem was never that good subcontractors did not exist.

The problem was that there was no trusted, easy, local way to find them when you actually needed them.

TradeSource was born from that realization:
build a vetted contractor network where contractors with overflow work can post jobs at fixed rates and connect with other vetted contractors who are ready to take on the work.

---

## Why This Matters Beyond One Job

This was not just about one bad day or one subcontractor not showing up.

It exposed a bigger gap in construction:

- contractors do not have a trusted labor network when they need one
- good subcontractors struggle to get consistent visibility
- younger operators want to grow without building large in-house crews
- the industry still relies too heavily on panic, guesswork, and random labor sourcing

TradeSource exists because that system is broken.

---

## Founder Belief

I believe construction needs the same kind of friction removal that other industries have already seen.

Uber reduced friction in ride sharing.
Amazon reduced friction in shopping.
Carvana reduced friction in car buying.
Opendoor reduced friction in home transactions.

TradeSource is built to reduce friction in how construction work gets shared, staffed, and fulfilled.

---

## Short Version

TradeSource started when I was in the middle of a five-figure repaint job, my regular subcontractor no-showed on day two, and I found myself alone, scrambling through Facebook Marketplace, Google, and paint stores trying to find labor I could trust.

Sitting in my truck, I realized there should be a better way.

That idea became TradeSource: a vetted painting contractor network where contractors can post overflow work at fixed rates and connect with trusted subs instead of relying on panic and guesswork.
