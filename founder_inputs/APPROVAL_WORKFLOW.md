## Who Approves

The only approver is the founder.

The founder is TradeSource.

---

## Approval Flow

**Step 1 — Draft:** Content is drafted and placed in the draft folder.

**Step 2 — Submit for Review:** Send the founder a Telegram message stating the draft is ready to review. Include the content type and what channel it is intended for.

**Step 3 — Founder Review:** The founder reviews the draft.

**Step 4 — Approval:** The founder responds with the exact word: **approved**

**Step 5 — Publish:** Upon receiving "approved", Hermes may post the content as directed.

---

## What Does NOT Count As Approval

The following phrases do **not** authorize publication. Do not treat these as approval under any circumstances:

- "nice work"
- "looks good"
- "very nice"
- "pretty good"
- "good job"
- "interesting"
- "not bad"
- "go ahead"
- "sounds fine"
- "I see it"
- "acknowledged"
- silence

**Only the exact word "approved" counts.**

If the founder responds with anything other than the word "approved" — including follow-up questions, revision requests, or silence — do not publish. Treat any ambiguous response as a pending. Ask for clarification before treating anything as approved.

---

## Telegram Communication Guidelines

When submitting a draft for review, the Telegram message should include:
- Content type (e.g., "Instagram post", "email draft", "website copy")
- Target channel
- A clear statement that the draft is ready for review
- Location of the draft if not in the standard folder

**Example submission message:**
> "Draft ready for review: Instagram post — overflow labor pain point. Target: Instagram. Folder: ~/drafts/instagram/"

---

## What Happens If a Draft Needs Changes

If the founder responds with revision feedback, treat the revised draft as a new submission. Do not assume the next "approved" applies automatically — always wait for the explicit word.
